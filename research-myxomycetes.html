Research · Topic

Slime moulds (Myxomycetes)

Myxomycetes (Amoebozoa) are a group of protists, comprising more than 1,050 described species1. They are almost cosmopolitan in distribution and often abundant on the upper soil layer, yet their ecological roles remain relatively poorly understood.

My research focuses on the phylogeography and genetic diversity of nivicolous myxomycetes — species that form fruiting bodies near snowbanks shortly after the seasonal snow cover (typically lasting over three months) has melted.

This ecological guild is particularly suitable for quantitative studies, as seasonal sampling is predictable, and most species belong to the dark-spored clade, which is particularly well-suited for molecular identification using the nearly universal 18S rDNA barcoding marker2,3,4.

Although these organisms cannot yet be cultivated under laboratory conditions, molecular data from field-collected specimens provide valuable insights into the reproductive strategies of free-dispersing protists and patterns of local adaptation from an evolutionary biology perspective.

Current projects

  1. Phylogeography of nivicolous myxomycetes in the mountains of Eurasia.
  2. Genetic diversity of nivicolous myxomycetes in Japan.

Collaborative work

I am also involved in several collaborative projects on nivicolous myxomycetes in various mountain ranges, as well as on taxonomy, to which I contribute by conducting investigations and analysing data.

Within the lab

  • Nivicolous myxomycetes in the French Pyrenees. Investigation · species accumulation curve · writing (review & editing)
  • Population genetics of Diderma meyerae across European mountains. Investigation · data curation
  • Nivicolous myxomycetes in Macedonia, Romania, the French Alps, and the German Alps (Berchtesgaden). Data curation · data evaluation
  • Taxonomic works: Meriderma spp., Badhamia almatensis, Diderma brooksii, Tubifera spp., Reticularia spp. Investigation · data curation · data evaluation

With external colleagues

  • Long-term research on nivicolous myxomycetes in the German Alps and the Tatra Mountains — with Dr. A. Ronikier and Dr. P. Janik, Polish Academy of Sciences. Investigation · data curation · morphological identification
  • Nivicolous myxomycetes in Kazakhstan — with PhD cand. Z. Azirakhmet, L.N. Gumilyov Eurasian National University. Morphological identification · writing (review & editing)
  • Corticolous myxomycetes in Japan — with Dr. K. Fujioka. Data evaluation for the molecular part
  • Genetic diversity of myxomycetes in East Asia — with a Master’s student at National Taiwan University and many independent researchers in Japan and Taiwan. Joint investigation

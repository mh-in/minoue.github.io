Club mosses (Lycopodiaceae)

Genus Diphasiastrum is a plant group belonging to the oldest lineage of extant terrestrial plants, the class Lycopodiophyta (or Lycophyta)[1]. In Systematics, Diphasiastrum has been recognized as a section of the genus Lycopodium L. (Lycopodium sec. Complanata), but due to its some of its specific features (branchlet structure, chromosome number [n = 23], and carrot-shaped gametophytes), they are now treated as a genus Diphasiastrum[2]. With 27 described taxa[3], including hybrids, the most complex group within the Lycopodiaceae, Diphasiastrum is distributed widely throughout the Northern Hemisphere.

In contrast to other plants which are involved in more allopolyploid hybridization and consequently have more polyploid individuals, in Diphasiastrum, only a few polyploids have been recorded[4, 5, 6] and homoploid hybrid speciation (diploid homoploid species) is supposedly predominant, except for some hybrid taxa originating from polypoid hybridization (e.g. D. x wightianum D. x zanclophyllum or D. x yueshanense)[4]
